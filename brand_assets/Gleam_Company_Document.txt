1. Company Overview
What is Gleam?
Gleam is a real-time quality control system for small residential cleaning businesses in the United States. The platform enables cleaning company owners to monitor every job being done by their team — in real time — without being physically present at each property.
Cleaners complete photo-verified checklists on-site using their mobile phone. Owners watch progress live from a dashboard: they see which rooms are done, which items are pending, and photos as proof of work. Nothing is hidden, nothing is forgotten.
The Problem We Solve
Owners of cleaning businesses with 5 to 30 employees face an invisible but devastating problem: they cannot be in multiple homes simultaneously. Quality inconsistency causes silent client churn — customers do not complain, they simply cancel.
A client who pays $200/session and cancels represents $2,400/year in lost recurring revenue
Most owners have no system to track quality — they rely on WhatsApp, paper notes, or nothing
A single missed spot or forgotten task can end a client relationship permanently
Competitors like ZenMaid, Swept, and Janitorial Manager focus on scheduling — not accountability
Gleam is the first product in this market focused specifically on photo-verified quality control per checklist item. We do not schedule jobs. We make cleaners accountable for every single task.
The Magic Moment
The first time an owner opens the Gleam dashboard and sees a cleaner completing checklist items with photos — in real time — from three different homes simultaneously. For the first time, they have eyes everywhere.
This moment typically happens within the first job created. It is fast, visceral, and impossible to forget. That is when the product sells itself.

2. Product
How It Works
Owner Flow
Owner signs up and creates their company account (14-day free trial, no credit card required)
Owner adds properties: each property has a client name, address, and a custom checklist organized by room (Kitchen, Bathroom, Living Room, etc.)
Each room has individual checklist items (e.g., Kitchen: clean countertops, mop floor, clean sink)
Owner creates jobs: assign a cleaner to a property on a specific date
Owner monitors the live dashboard: see progress bars, status badges, and photos as cleaners work
Owner can flag issues, see which items were missed, and send feedback to the cleaner
Cleaner Flow
Cleaner receives an invite link from the owner and creates their account
Cleaner opens the mobile app and sees all jobs scheduled for today
Cleaner arrives at the property and taps 'Start Job'
For each checklist item, the cleaner takes a photo as proof and marks it complete
Progress bar updates in real time — owner sees every action as it happens
When all items are done, cleaner taps 'Complete Job' — the job is marked done
Key Features
Feature	Description
Photo-verified checklists	Every checklist item requires a photo as proof of completion. No photo = not done.
Real-time owner dashboard	Live progress view showing all jobs across all properties simultaneously.
Custom checklists by room	Owners build checklists per room per property. Fully customizable.
Mobile-first cleaner app	Designed for use with wet/dirty hands — large buttons, generous spacing.
Team management	Invite cleaners via link, track their active jobs, remove them anytime.
Multi-tenant isolation	Each company sees only their own data. Row-level security enforced at the database level.
Stripe billing	Self-serve subscription with trial, plan upgrade/downgrade, and overage detection.
Google OAuth	Sign in with Google for fast, secure authentication.
Tech Stack
Frontend + Backend: Next.js 16 with App Router, React 19, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Turbopack
Database: Supabase PostgreSQL with Row Level Security (RLS)
Auth: Supabase Auth (email/password + Google OAuth)
Storage: Supabase Storage — bucket 'job-photos' for cleaner photo uploads
Payments: Stripe (Checkout, Customer Portal, Webhooks)
Deploy: Vercel (hobby plan, auto-deploy on push to GitHub)
Domain: gleamqc.com (Namecheap, DNS pointing to Vercel)
Database Schema
Six tables with full multi-tenant isolation via org_id:
organizations — company account, plan, trial dates, Stripe IDs
profiles — user accounts linked to an org, role: owner or cleaner
properties — client homes with address and checklist templates
checklist_templates — JSONB array of rooms and items per property
jobs — scheduled cleaning tasks linking a property, cleaner, and date
job_items — individual checklist items within a job, with photo URL and completion status

4. Pricing Strategy
Model
Flat fee per tier based on number of active cleaners. Simple to understand for a non-tech-savvy ICP. Three tiers, monthly and annual billing, 14-day free trial with no credit card required.
Pricing Tiers
Plan	Cleaners	Monthly	Annual	Annual /mo
Starter	Up to 5	$49/mo	$490/yr	~$41/mo
Growth (Most Popular)	Up to 15	$99/mo	$990/yr	~$83/mo
Pro	Unlimited	$179/mo	$1,790/yr	~$149/mo
Annual discount: 2 months free (~16.7% off). Pushed at end of trial: 'Lock in your rate — 2 months free.'
Overage: $9/cleaner/month for accounts that temporarily exceed their tier limit. Captures incremental revenue and signals a natural upgrade path without requiring an immediate plan change.
Pricing Rationale
Why $49 for Starter?
Removes entry friction for skeptical 1 to 5 cleaner shops who have never paid for software
Land-and-expand entry point — most accounts will grow past 5 cleaners and upgrade naturally
Low enough that a single retained client ROI argument is obvious
Why $99 for Growth?
Sits just under $100 — psychologically important for non-tech buyers
Covers the ICP sweet spot: 5 to 15 cleaners, where ~70% of accounts will land
Value capture ~16% of the $600+/month value delivered — well within B2B SaaS benchmarks
Competitors charge $50 to $200 for less specialized tools, making $99 feel fair and premium
Why $179 for Pro?
Anchors perceived value at the top, captures larger operators (15 to 30 cleaners)
Still below competitors' high-end pricing ($200), positioning Gleam as specialized but fair
API access + dedicated onboarding justify the price for operators running at scale

5. Go-to-Market Strategy
Motion: Product-Led Growth (PLG)
Gleam is fully self-serve. No sales team at launch. The product sells itself through the trial experience. Owners sign up, create their first job, see the real-time dashboard update as a cleaner completes items — and the value is immediately obvious.
Distribution Channels
Primary: Facebook Groups
Facebook has hundreds of active groups for residential cleaning business owners in the U.S.
Groups like 'Cleaning Business Owners' and 'House Cleaning Business Tips' have 50,000+ members
Strategy: genuine value-first posting — share insights about quality control, client retention, team management — not direct ads
Soft CTAs pointing to gleamqc.com and the 14-day free trial
Secondary: Cleaning Industry Communities
Reddit communities (r/CleaningBusinessOwners, r/Entrepreneur)
YouTube comments on cleaning business channels
Nextdoor and local business Facebook groups
Tertiary: Referral Loop
Every time a cleaner gets invited to Gleam, they see the product from the inside
Cleaners who leave one company may start their own and become owners (Gleam customers)
Built-in organic referral: owners who love Gleam talk to other owners in their networks
Trial-to-Paid Conversion
14-day trial, no credit card required — maximum top-of-funnel volume
At end of trial: billing wall appears, owner must choose a plan to continue
Annual plan nudge at trial end: 'Lock in your rate — 2 months free'
Target conversion rate: 25% trial-to-paid (typical for simple self-serve B2B SaaS)
